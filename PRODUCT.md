# Product

## Register

brand

## Users
Owner-operators and small-team owners of cleaning businesses (residential maid services, house cleaning, move-in/move-out, Airbnb/turnover, light commercial/janitorial), typically running the business from their phone between jobs. They've outgrown spreadsheets and group texts but assume "real software" is expensive and built for plumbers/HVAC, not for them.

## Product Purpose
The customer-facing marketing landing page (`/`) for JobDock — software that runs the whole cleaning job from first text to final payment: quotes, recurring scheduling, online booking, job logs with before/after photos + time tracking, client CRM, reports, and card/ACH payments (QuickBooks + Stripe). The page's job is to convince a cleaning-business owner to start a free account ($29/mo).

## Brand Personality
Confident, plain-spoken, practical. Fresh and clean (the product's promise made visual): bright, airy, sunlit. Not corporate, not cutesy. Voice is specific and concrete, never buzzword marketing. Emotional goal: relief ("this finally fits how I actually work") and trust ("I'll get paid").

## Anti-references
- The previous JobDock site: dark navy + gold, contractor/trades framing. This rebuild is a deliberate departure.
- Generic AI SaaS landing: cream/sand body, uppercase tracked eyebrow above every section, identical icon-card grids, hero-metric stat blocks, decorative glassmorphism.
- Over-corporate fintech (navy/gold gradients) and over-cute consumer pastel.

## Design Principles
- **Show the product working.** Real app screens in device mockups beat abstract promises.
- **Clean is the brand.** Brightness, whitespace, and real sunlit cleaning imagery carry the "fresh/clean" message; the medium is the message.
- **Phone-first.** The audience lives on their phone; mobile is the primary canvas, not an afterthought.
- **Specific over slogan.** Every claim maps to a real feature; concrete nouns and verbs over marketing cadence.
- **Immersion is punctuation.** Parallax/pinned moments are deliberate beats between calm, readable sections, not a constant effect.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target WCAG AA: body text ≥4.5:1, large text ≥3:1. Full `prefers-reduced-motion` support (parallax/reveals disable). No horizontal overflow at any width. Mobile touch targets ≥44px.
